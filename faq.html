FAQ & operations

How the MVP works

Clean explanations for buyers, sellers, and admins — because confusion is not a feature.

How do I bid?

In the real product, you would create an account, pass any required verification, and place bids on eligible lots. In this static MVP, the UI demonstrates the flow without backend bidding logic.

How do I pay if I win?

For the MVP, buyers are expected to complete payment by bank transfer within 48 hours. An admin then manually confirms the payment before release, pickup scheduling, or shipping.

Can Macau buyers collect in person?

Yes — local pickup is a strong MVP option. It reduces shipping friction, makes manual confirmation easier, and gives the platform an extra trust signal.

What happens if a seller submits expensive cards?

High-value cards should go through extra checks: identity verification, photo review, reserve discussion, and potentially provenance evidence for especially sensitive lots.

Is the marketplace separate from auctions?

Functionally yes, but they should connect. Unsold auction lots can move to marketplace inventory, and marketplace data can help estimate auction reserves.

Recommended next step

If this prototype becomes real

The next layer should be a lightweight backend for authentication, bid events, invoice generation, admin moderation, and manual payment tracking. Keep the clean front end; add boring but essential systems underneath it. Glamour without operations is how marketplaces become apology generators.

Must-have production modules

  • Auth + bidder eligibility
  • Server-side bid validation
  • Order / invoice tracking
  • Manual payment state management
  • Seller payout records
