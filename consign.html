Seller onboarding

Consign cards with clear expectations.

This page is built to make sellers comfortable fast: what you can submit, how review works, how payouts happen, and which guardrails protect buyers and the platform.

Consignment interest form

What sellers want to know

  • Review before approval to auction or marketplace
  • High-value items may need extra identity proof
  • Clear reserve discussion before listing goes live
  • Payout after buyer payment is confirmed
  • Unsold items can move into marketplace inventory
How it works

A better seller flow for the MVP

1. IntakeSeller shares photos, estimates, and preferred sale method. Staff decides auction vs marketplace fit.
2. ReviewAuthenticity, condition, and pricing are reviewed manually before publication.
3. ListingApproved cards are published into a scheduled auction or buy-now catalog.
4. PayoutOnce buyer payment is manually confirmed and the order is released, seller payout is initiated on the agreed schedule.

Suggested MVP policies

  • Consignor agreement stored before go-live
  • Basic anti-shill review for linked bidder accounts
  • Photo standards for sealed and raw cards
  • Pickup logging for in-person drop-off and collection
Macau payout reality

Simple payment rails at launch

For launch, seller payouts can stay manual too: bank transfer after buyer funds are confirmed. It is boring, yes. It is also realistic, cheap to operate, and far less likely to explode in week one.

Admin control points

  • Hold payout until payment clears
  • Track proof-of-payment references
  • Flag risky sellers or disputed lots
  • Record all release/payout timestamps
