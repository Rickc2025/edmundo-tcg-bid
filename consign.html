What sellers want to know

  • Review before approval to auction or marketplace
  • High-value items may need extra identity proof
  • Clear reserve discussion before listing goes live
  • Payout after buyer payment is confirmed
  • Unsold items can move into marketplace inventory
How it works

A seller flow that feels orderly and professional

1. IntakeThe seller shares photos, estimates, and preferred sale method. Staff then recommends auction or marketplace placement.
2. ReviewAuthenticity, condition, and pricing are reviewed manually before publication.
3. ListingApproved cards are presented in a scheduled auction or a buy-now catalog.
4. PayoutAfter buyer payment is confirmed and the order is released, seller payout follows the agreed schedule.

Suggested service policies

  • Consignor agreement completed before go-live
  • Basic anti-shill review for linked bidder accounts
  • Photo standards for sealed and raw cards
  • Pickup logging for in-person drop-off and collection
Payout flow

Simple payment rails keep launch operations practical

Seller payouts can be handled by bank transfer after buyer funds are confirmed. It is straightforward, cost-efficient, and easy for staff to track.

Admin control points

  • Hold payout until payment clears
  • Track proof-of-payment references
  • Flag risky sellers or disputed lots
  • Record release and payout timestamps
